Massachusetts law requires all open session meetings of public bodies to be accessible to members of the public, including people with disabilities.

Need an Accommodation? Contact us.

Please contact the Town of Arlington ADA Coordinator as soon as possible:

  • Email: dei@town.arlington.ma.us
  • Phone: 781-316-3257

For interpretation or CART: The Massachusetts Office on Disability recommends requesting services at least 2 weeks before a meeting. Requests made closer to the meeting will still be considered, but services may not be possible to arrange in time.

Step 1: What Can You Request?

A reasonable accommodation is a change to a policy, practice, communication method, or process so a person with a disability can participate equally in Town meetings, programs, and services.

Common Examples

  • ASL interpretation or CART/live captioning
  • Large-print or accessible digital materials
  • Remote access to the meeting via video or teleconference
  • Accessible seating or route adjustments in a meeting space
  • Reasonable schedule or format adjustments when feasible

Step 2: Include Key Details

  • The meeting, service, program, or public space involved
  • Date, time, and location (if known)
  • The accommodation you are requesting
  • How the request relates to access needs
  • The best way to contact you for follow-up

Step 3: What Happens Next

The Town, with the ADA Coordinator, reviews requests and works to provide accommodations in a timely, good-faith way. Timing can vary depending on the type of service requested.

If a specific request cannot be provided as asked, the Town may work with you on an effective alternative access option.

Privacy and Documentation

Only information needed to process an accommodation request should be shared. In some cases, limited documentation may be requested when a disability-related need is not apparent, but unnecessary medical details should not be required.
